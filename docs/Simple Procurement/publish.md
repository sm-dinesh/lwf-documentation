# Publish Simple Procurement

## Overview

After creating a Simple Procurement, it is saved in a **Draft** state by default.  
In this state, the procurement is not visible to suppliers and cannot receive quotations.

To make the procurement active and visible, it must be **published**.

---

## Accessing the Publish Button

At the top of the procurement detail page, you will find the **Publish** button.  
This button is only available when the procurement is in Draft status.

![Publish Draft Button](/img/simple_procurement/simple_procurement_publish_draft.png)

---

## Steps to Publish

1. Open the Simple Procurement record in **Draft** status
2. Navigate to the top of the page  
3. Click on the **Publish** button  
4. Confirm the action if a confirmation prompt appears  

---

## After Publishing

Once the procurement is published:

- ✅ Status changes from **Draft → Published**
- ✅ Procurement becomes visible to suppliers
- ✅ Vendors can start submitting quotations
- ✅ The procurement process becomes active
- ✅ Submission deadline countdown begins

---

## Important Notes

⚠️ **Before publishing, ensure:**

- All procurement details are accurate
- Dates are correct and in logical sequence
- Required documents are attached
- Evaluation criteria are properly configured
- Vendor invitations will be sent (or will be sent after publishing)

⚠️ **After publishing:**

- Some fields may become **locked** from editing
- Published procurements may not be easily reverted to draft
- Deadlines and terms cannot be changed arbitrarily
- All changes are logged for audit purposes

---

## Next Steps

After publishing your procurement:

1. **[Invite Vendors](./simple-procurement-invitations)** – Send invitations to suppliers
2. **Review Submissions** – Monitor quotations as they arrive
3. **Evaluate Bids** – Use evaluation criteria to compare quotations
4. **Award Contract** – Select winning vendor and issue purchase order
