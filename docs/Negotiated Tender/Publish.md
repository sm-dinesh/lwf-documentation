# Publish Negotiated Tender

## Overview

After creating a Negotiated Tender, it is saved in a **Draft** state by default.  
In this state, the tender is not visible to invited suppliers and negotiations cannot begin.

To make the tender active and ready for supplier engagement, it must be **published**.

---

## Accessing the Publish Button

At the top of the tender detail page, you will find the **Publish** button.  
This button is only available when the tender is in Draft status.

---

## Steps to Publish

1. Open the Negotiated Tender record in **Draft** status
2. Navigate to the top of the page  
3. Click on the **Publish** button  
4. Confirm the action if a confirmation prompt appears  

---

## After Publishing

Once the tender is published:

- ✅ Status changes from **Draft → Published**
- ✅ Tender becomes visible to invited suppliers
- ✅ Suppliers can access the tender and submit proposals
- ✅ Negotiation process can begin
- ✅ Timeline for proposal submission becomes active

---

## Important Notes

⚠️ **Before publishing, ensure:**

- All tender details are accurate and complete
- Dates are correct and realistic
- Required tender documents are attached
- Evaluation criteria are properly configured
- Negotiation scope and parameters are clear
- Selected suppliers have been identified (or plan to invite after publishing)

⚠️ **After publishing:**

- Some fields may become **locked** from editing
- Published tenders may not be easily reverted to draft
- Submission deadlines should be honored
- All changes are logged for audit purposes
- Maintain clear communication with all suppliers

---

## Negotiation-Specific Considerations

💡 **Key Points:**

- After publishing, you will send **direct invitations** to specific suppliers
- Each invited supplier receives a unique copy of the tender
- Suppliers can begin preparing proposals
- Multiple rounds of discussion may occur
- Timeline is more flexible than open tendering

---

## Next Steps

After publishing your tender:

1. **[Send Bid Invitations](./bid-invitations)** – Invite selected suppliers  
2. **Prepare for Proposals** – Notify suppliers and begin engagement
3. **Monitor Proposal Submissions** – Track incoming proposals
4. **Review Proposals** – Assess proposal completeness and quality
5. **Start Negotiations** – Engage suppliers in detailed discussions
6. **Final Evaluation** – Score proposals using defined criteria
7. **Award Contract** – Select preferred supplier and negotiate final terms

---

## Timeline Flexibility

| Phase | Timeline | Flexibility |
|---|---|---|
| Proposal Submission | 2-4 weeks | Can be extended by mutual agreement |
| Evaluation | 1-2 weeks | Allows for supplier feedback rounds |
| Negotiation | 2-8 weeks | Highly flexible based on complexity |
| Award | After agreement | Depends on negotiation progress |

---

## Key Differences: Negotiated vs. Open Publishing

| Aspect | Negotiated | Open |
|---|---|---|
| Visibility | Only invited suppliers | Public/all bidders |
| Bidder Count | Limited & targeted | Unrestricted |
| Proposal Submission | Direct invitation required | Can participate openly |
| Timeline | Flexible, negotiable | Fixed |
| Communication | Direct, ongoing | Formal, limited |
| Goal | Best fit agreement | Best evaluated bid |
