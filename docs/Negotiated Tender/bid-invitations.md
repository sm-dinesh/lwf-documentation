# Bid Invitations

## Overview

The **Bid Invitations** feature is essential for Negotiated Tenders. It allows procurement managers to send direct invitations to specifically selected suppliers to participate and submit proposals.

Unlike Open Tenders where participation is unrestricted, Negotiated Tenders **require direct invitations** to specific suppliers. This targeted approach enables:

- Direct engagement with pre-selected capable suppliers
- Confidential negotiation discussions
- Flexible proposal and contract terms
- Relationship-based procurement

All invitation management is accessed through the tender **List Page** or **Edit Page**.

---

## Accessing Invitations

### From Tender List Page

- Click the **Invitation Button** next to a tender to manage bid invitations  

### From Tender Edit Page

- Click the **Invitation Button** on the edit page to open the invitation list  

---

## Invitation List Page

This page displays:

- All suppliers invited to the tender  
- Current status of each invited supplier  
- Proposal submission status for each supplier  
- Options to add new supplier invitations  
- Quick access to proposal evaluation tools

---

## Inviting Suppliers

### Step 1: Click Invite Supplier Button

- Click the **Invite Supplier** button to open the invitation modal

### Step 2: Select Supplier or Enter Email

Inside the modal:

1. **Select Supplier or Type Email** – Choose from existing registered suppliers or type a new email address
2. **Send Button** – Sends the invitation to the selected supplier(s)

---

## Supplier Status Tracking

The invitations list displays important information:

- **Supplier Details** – Name, contact, and company information
- **Invitation Status** – Whether invitation has been sent
- **Proposal Status** – Whether supplier has submitted proposal
- **Proposal Completeness** – Whether proposal includes all required documents
- **Evaluation Status** – Whether proposal has been evaluated
- **Contact Status** – Last communication date
- **Quick Actions** – Evaluation button and messaging tools

## Invitation Process

### Before Sending Invitations

1. **Tender should be published** – Ensure all details are finalized
2. **Identify suppliers** – Select appropriate suppliers for the scope
3. **Verify contact information** – Ensure accurate email addresses
4. **Document justification** – Record why each supplier was selected

### After Sending Invitations

1. **Monitor acknowledgments** – Confirm suppliers received invitations
2. **Track proposal status** – Monitor submission progress
3. **Send reminders** – Follow up on upcoming deadlines
4. **Prepare for discussion** – Review proposals as they arrive
5. **Begin negotiations** – Engage suppliers in detailed discussions

---

## Important Notes

⚠️ **Mandatory Invitations:**
- Bid invitations are **mandatory** for Negotiated Tenders
- At least one supplier must be invited to proceed
- Without invitations, negotiated process cannot begin

⚠️ **Supplier Selection:**
- Document selection criteria and justification
- Ensure selection is fair and well-reasoned
- Maintain audit trail of supplier selection process
- Consider competition factors

⚠️ **Confidentiality:**
- Treat supplier information as confidential
- Don't disclose competing supplier information
- Maintain supplier privacy throughout process
- Secure all proposal documentation

⚠️ **Communication:**
- Maintain professional communication
- Provide clear, consistent information to all suppliers
- Respond to supplier queries promptly
- Document all communications

## Negotiation Phase

After suppliers receive invitations and submit proposals:

1. **Review Initial Proposals** – Assess proposal quality and completeness
2. **Preliminary Feedback** – Identify areas for discussion
3. **Schedule Discussions** – Plan negotiation meetings
4. **Conduct Negotiations** – Discuss terms, pricing, and conditions
5. **Refine Terms** – Adjust proposals based on discussions
6. **Final Evaluation** – Score refined proposals
7. **Award** – Select preferred supplier and execute contract

## Key Differences from Open Tender Invitations

| Aspect | Negotiated Bids | Open Tender Bids |
|---|---|---|
| Requirement | **Mandatory** | Optional |
| Supplier Count | Limited & targeted | Unrestricted |
| Participation Model | By invitation only | Open to all |
| Communication | Direct & ongoing | Formal notices only |
| Process | Flexible & collaborative | Fixed & competitive |
| Goal | Best-fit partnership | Best-evaluated bid |
